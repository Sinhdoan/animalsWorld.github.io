Sea Animals

The blue ocean is home to all sorts of marine life, from microscopic microalgae to the largest animal on the planet, the blue whale.

  • Turtle

    The order Turtles (scientific name: Testudines) are reptiles belonging to the crown group of the superorder Chelonia (or Testudinata).

    Learn more

  • Squid

    The molluscs in the superorder Decapodiformes (superorder Ten-legged) and part of the superorder Octopodiformes (superorder Octopus).

    Learn more

  • Shark

    Is a group of fish in the class Cartilaginous fish, with a hydrodynamic body that easily displaces water, with 5 to 7 gill slits along each side or near the head (the first slot behind the eye is called the spiracle), the skin has many small spines covering coat the body against parasites, the rows of teeth in the mouth can grow back.
